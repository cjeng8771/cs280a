💡Thoughts

The distorted effect in the close up image is a result of the short distance between the camera lens and Abby's face. When the lens is that close to the object being photographed, individual objects that are closer to the lens will appear disproportionately larger than surrounding objects. As the camera is moved further from Abby's face, there is less perspective distortion on her facial features, allowing the portraits to look closer to normal.

💡Thoughts

The first image, far down the street and zoomed in, appears flatter than the succeeding street views because of perspective compression. When the focus is further away from the camera, depth perception is altered and the difference in perceived size of adjacent objects is reduced.
